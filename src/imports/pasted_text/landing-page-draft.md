━━━ DESIGN PRINCIPLES ━━━

WHITESPACE:
  Every section has one job. Padding between sections: minimum 96px.
  The page should feel like it has room to breathe — not a brochure.

HERO H1:
  Positive, forward-looking. Three short lines. No fear or stress words.
  "Shine" is the emotional peak — protect it.

HERO SUBHEADING:
  "Adaptive practice and a clear path to exam day" must stay verbatim.
  These are the two core product features. Everything else on the page
  supports and proves these two phrases.

BRAND HERO IMAGE (Section 2, right column):
  The path arc of 20 nodes IS the product story. It must be the most
  visually distinctive element on the page. A parent glancing at it
  should immediately understand: there is a structured plan here,
  and my child is on it. Not a floating badge collection.

FEATURE SPOTLIGHT (Section 5):
  The adaptive insight card ("Based on Emma's results, we've adjusted...")
  is the single most important copy block on the page after the H1.
  It proves the adaptive claim concretely. It must be legible
  and prominent inside the illustration.

SECTION TITLES:
  Max 7 words. Benefit-led. No exclamation marks.

TESTIMONIAL QUOTES:
  One sentence only. The emerald school pill carries the credibility.
  Do not lengthen the quotes.

PRICING FEATURE LIST:
  Line 1 explicitly names "Adaptive learning path" — this is intentional.
  The pricing section is where parents commit; remind them of the
  core differentiator at the moment of decision.
